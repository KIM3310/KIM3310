## 1. Korean Tech Communities and Meetups

### Snowflake Korea Community
- **What:** Official Snowflake user community in Korea; hosts workshops, webinars, and networking events
- **Action:** Join the Snowflake Korea Community (check Snowflake's community page and Meetup.com)
- **Goal:** Attend at least one event before applying; ask informed questions that demonstrate SnowPro-level knowledge
- **Talking Point:** Share experience building Nexus-Hive's multi-warehouse adapter pattern

### Databricks Korea User Group
- **What:** Korean user community for Databricks practitioners
- **Action:** Find and join via Databricks community forums, Meetup, or LinkedIn groups
- **Goal:** Present or offer a lightning talk on lakehouse-contract-lab or the MLflow experiment tracking system
- **Talking Point:** Dual-cloud Platform Architect perspective (AWS+GCP)

### AWSKRUG (AWS Korea User Group)
- **What:** One of the largest and most active tech communities in Korea
- **Action:** Join relevant sub-groups (Data Analytics, AI/ML, Architecture)
- **Where:** meetup.com/awskrug or Facebook groups
- **Goal:** Participate in monthly meetups; consider presenting on Databricks-on-AWS architecture

### GDG (Google Developer Group) Korea
- **What:** Google-affiliated developer communities in major Korean cities
- **Action:** Join GDG Busan (local) and GDG Seoul (for larger events)
- **Goal:** Attend Cloud and AI/ML focused sessions; network with Google Cloud practitioners

### Seoul Data Science Meetup / PyData Seoul
- **What:** Data science and Python communities hosting regular talks
- **Action:** Attend meetups; propose a talk on NL-to-SQL systems (Nexus-Hive)
- **Goal:** Build visibility in the data science community

### MLOps Korea
- **What:** Community focused on ML operations and production ML systems
- **Action:** Join and participate in discussions about MLflow, experiment tracking, model deployment
- **Goal:** Share enterprise-llm-adoption-kit as a case study

### Open Source Korea Communities
- **What:** Various open source communities (OSS Korea, Contributhon)
- **Action:** Participate as a contributor; stage-pilot demonstrates existing open-source commitment
- **Goal:** Build reputation as an active open-source contributor

## 5. Informational Interview Questions

When you secure a coffee chat or informational interview, use these questions:

1. "What does a typical day or week look like for an SE/SA at [Company] Korea?"
2. "What skills or experiences have been most valuable in your role?"
3. "How does the Korea team collaborate with global teams?"
4. "What are the biggest challenges Korean enterprise customers face when adopting [platform]?"
5. "What advice would you give someone preparing to interview for an SE/SA role at [Company]?"
6. "Are there any skills or certifications that are particularly valued internally?"
7. "How does the team handle the balance between technical depth and customer-facing work?"
