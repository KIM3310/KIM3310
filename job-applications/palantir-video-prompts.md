# Palantir Video Prompt Drafts

Verified prompt source: official Palantir Seoul application forms on `2026-03-28`

The forms request two unlisted YouTube videos, each up to 90 seconds:

1. `Show us something you've built that is important to you. What, why, and how did you create it?`
2. `Tell us about a piece of Palantir coverage or content you find thought-provoking and why?`

These drafts are intentionally conversational rather than fully scripted.

## Prompt 1 draft

### Best project to use

`AegisOps`

### 90-second structure

1. Start with the problem
   - "One system that's important to me is AegisOps, an incident analysis workflow I built."
2. Explain why it matters
   - "I built it because I wanted to solve a real operational problem: when an incident happens, people are overwhelmed with scattered evidence, but they still need to make fast and trustworthy decisions."
3. Explain what it does
   - "The system takes logs and screenshots, turns them into structured review, and produces replayable postmortem output."
4. Explain how you built it
   - "I designed it as an operator-facing workflow, not just a model demo. That meant evaluation, review surfaces, handoff, and clear evidence trails had to be part of the product."
5. Close with why it represents you
   - "It matters to me because it shows how I like to build: close to real workflows, with ambiguity, and with software that helps users act rather than just observe."

## Prompt 2 draft

### Recommended content to discuss

Use the official Palantir role pages, especially the `Deployment Strategist` description, because it closely matches your portfolio.

Recommended link:

- https://jobs.lever.co/palantir/2e193bdf-e668-49c1-ba32-66cc1aa131a4

### 90-second structure

1. Identify the content
   - "The Palantir content I found most thought-provoking was the Deployment Strategist role description."
2. Explain what stood out
   - "What stood out was the idea of taking disconnected signals from users, data, and workflows and turning them into a clear understanding of the most important problem."
3. Connect to your own work
   - "That resonated with me because my strongest systems come from exactly that kind of ambiguity. They start with messy operational pain, not clean benchmark tasks."
4. Give a concrete example
   - "In projects like AegisOps or my review-gated workflow systems, the challenge was not just building software. It was understanding which decision needed to become clearer for the user."
5. Close
   - "That is why the content stood out to me. It describes software work that is deeply technical, but still grounded in user reality and operational impact."

## Delivery notes

- Speak slowly and naturally.
- Do not memorize every sentence.
- Keep eye contact steady and energy calm.
- For Prompt 1, emphasize what you learned from building the system.
- For Prompt 2, emphasize why the content changed or sharpened how you think about the role.
