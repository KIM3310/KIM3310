# Interview Preparation: Palantir Korea FDSE / Deployment Strategist

## Core Positioning

Position yourself as:

> a builder who thrives in ambiguous, operationally important environments and can move from messy workflows to working systems fast.

This is not a generic "AI engineer" interview. It is a **field problem-solving** interview.

---

## Likely Interview Themes

1. Open-ended problem solving
2. User empathy and workflow understanding
3. Coding / architecture fluency
4. Ownership in ambiguous environments
5. Korean + English communication in high-context settings

---

## Topic 1: Why You Fit Palantir

**Best answer shape**

- You do not just build models or APIs.
- You build systems where evidence, approvals, handoff, and action all matter.
- Your strongest examples are operational and workflow-heavy.

**Best proof examples**

- `AegisOps`
- `memory-test-master-change-gate`
- `ops-reliability-workbench`
- `regulated-case-workbench`

---

## Topic 2: How You Handle Ambiguity

**Likely Questions**

- Tell me about a time you had to solve an unclear problem.
- How do you find the real user problem when stakeholders give incomplete answers?
- What do you do when requirements change constantly?

**Answer Framework**

1. Identify the actual user workflow.
2. Find the decision bottleneck, not just the technical symptom.
3. Build the smallest useful surface first.
4. Use real feedback loops quickly.
5. Expand only after the workflow proves useful.

---

## Topic 3: How You Think About Data + Product

**Likely Questions**

- How do you connect messy data to useful decisions?
- What matters more: perfect data or useful workflow integration?

**Talking Points**

- Data quality matters, but software becomes valuable when it changes the next decision.
- You prefer systems that expose confidence, evidence, and ownership rather than pretending certainty.
- Your best projects turn operational data into review, escalation, or release decisions.

---

## Topic 4: Technical Interview / Case Discussion

**Likely Scenario**

"A Korean enterprise customer has fragmented data and unclear operational pain. What do you do first?"

**Answer Framework**

1. Map users, workflows, and decisions.
2. Identify the smallest high-leverage operational loop.
3. Build a thin working slice with real data and real users.
4. Add auditability and ownership early.
5. Use iterative deployment, not giant up-front architecture.

---

## Topic 5: What Not to Do

- Do not over-index on model buzzwords.
- Do not describe yourself as a pure researcher.
- Do not frame the portfolio as "many projects."
- Do frame it as a repeated pattern: operational ambiguity -> reviewable workflow -> deployable system.

---

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer

1. "For the Korea team, how much of the work is closer to FDSE versus Deployment Strategist in practice?"
2. "What kinds of customer workflows in Korea most often benefit from field-style product building?"
3. "How early do engineers and strategists get exposed to users and live workflows?"
4. "What distinguishes candidates who can thrive in Palantir's ambiguity-heavy environment?"
5. "How does the Korea team balance fast iteration with the trust requirements of large institutions?"
