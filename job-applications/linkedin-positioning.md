## About section

I build applied AI systems that are reliable enough for real workflows.

My strongest work sits between AI engineering and solution architecture: runtime reliability, governed analytics, enterprise AI rollout, and operator-facing delivery. I focus on systems that make failure modes visible, keep review boundaries explicit, and stay useful under operational scrutiny.

Representative work includes:

- `stage-pilot`: tool-calling reliability runtime that improved benchmark performance from 25% to 90%, published on npm
- `AegisOps`: multimodal incident analysis and postmortem system with replay evaluation
- `Nexus-Hive`: governed NL-to-SQL analytics across Snowflake and Databricks
- `enterprise-llm-adoption-kit`: enterprise AI adoption framework with RBAC, auditability, eval logging, and rollout controls
- `lakehouse-contract-lab`: Spark and Delta medallion pipeline with contract-driven quality gates

I hold certifications across Snowflake, Databricks, Palantir Foundry, Datadog, and Microsoft AI, and I work comfortably across Korean, English, and Japanese environments.

The shortest description of my strength is this: I do not just build AI systems that look impressive. I build AI systems that people can actually trust in practice.

## What to remove or de-emphasize

- Too many side projects in the top surface
- Generic “AI enthusiast” wording
- Research-style ambition that does not match the current strongest evidence
- Certification-first messaging without project proof
